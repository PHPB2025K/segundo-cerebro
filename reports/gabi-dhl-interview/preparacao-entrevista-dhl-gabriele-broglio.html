2. Pitch inicial — “Tell me about yourself”

Resposta em inglês

I have built my career around operations, customer-facing routines and cross-functional problem-solving. What I enjoy most is connecting the dots between the customer needs and the internal operation, making sure issues are followed up properly, root causes are understood, and the service keeps improving.

For this role, I believe my strengths are communication, organization, analytical thinking and the ability to work with different stakeholders under pressure. I see this position as a good match because it combines customer success, fulfillment operations, process improvement and daily coordination with teams such as customer service, warehouse, carriers, IT and commercial.

Ideia central: carreira ligada a operação + cliente + resolução estruturada. Não precisa decorar palavra por palavra.

3. Perguntas prováveis e respostas-base

Why are you interested in this role?

I am interested because the role is not only about answering customer questions. It is about understanding the operation behind the service, identifying recurring issues, aligning the right stakeholders and improving the customer experience in a sustainable way. This is exactly the type of environment where I believe I can add value: structured, dynamic, operational and customer-focused.

Tradução da intenção: “gosto porque mistura cliente + operação + melhoria real”.

How do you handle a dissatisfied customer or an escalation?

First, I make sure I understand the real issue and the impact for the customer. Then I separate what needs immediate containment from what requires root cause analysis. I align internally with the right stakeholders, define clear ownership and timelines, and keep the customer informed with transparent updates. After the situation is stabilized, I look for the root cause to prevent the same issue from happening again.

Palavras-chave: understand impact, containment, root cause, ownership, timeline, transparent updates.

Tell me about a time you solved a recurring problem.

A good way I approach recurring problems is by first mapping the pattern: when it happens, which team is involved, what the customer impact is, and what data confirms the issue. Then I bring the relevant people together, agree on the root cause and define a practical action plan. I also like to document the new process or template, because if the solution depends only on memory, the problem usually comes back.

Aqui ela deve encaixar um exemplo real dela. Se não lembrar um forte, use essa estrutura como resposta conceitual.

How do you work with multiple stakeholders?

I try to be very clear about roles, priorities and expected outcomes. Different stakeholders usually see the same problem from different angles, so my role is to translate the customer impact into operational actions. I like to keep communication objective, define who owns each action and follow up until the issue is closed.

How do you deal with pressure?

Under pressure, I try to stay practical. I prioritize what affects the customer or the operation the most, communicate clearly, and avoid creating noise. I believe pressure is easier to manage when there is structure: clear facts, clear owners and clear next steps.

What would you focus on in your first 90 days?

In the first 90 days, I would focus on understanding the product, the main customers, the service standards, the operational flows and the most frequent pain points. I would also try to build strong relationships with customer service, warehouse, carriers, IT, sales and project delivery. After that, I would look for quick wins: recurring issues, unclear templates, gaps in communication or small process improvements that could reduce friction for customers and internal teams.
